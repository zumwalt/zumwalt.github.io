Cross Browser Testing

An ongoing, iterative redesign of a web app.

Over the past two years, my team at Simple Focus has helped CrossBrowserTesting.com design and develop a world-class web app. I established the creative direction early on in the project, and we’re constantly iterating and improving the experience. In my time working with CBT, the app’s user base has more than doubled to nearly 90,000.

The problem

CrossBrowserTesting.com has always had a strong product — easy testing of websites across multiple operating systems and browsers from a single computer. When I began working on the product, the UI was clunky and outdated, with bits and pieces of open source design frameworks and icon packs scattered about. Launching a live test was overly complex and difficult to navigate. My first order of business was to dream up a simpler experience.

My process

I began the project on paper, sketching several ideas for the core functionality of the app at the time — choosing a browser and operating system to test.

My next step was diving head-first into the UI. Based around a common color palette, I explored both the aesthetic of the overall site as well as the specific interactions of the live test picker.

Bringing it together

In the end, the team at CrossBrowserTesting.com went with a simple UI that's still evolving today.
